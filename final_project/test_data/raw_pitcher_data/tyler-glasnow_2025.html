Tyler Allen Glasnow
Tyler Allen Glasnow…born Aug. 23, 1993 in Newhall, Calif…his mother, Donna, was a gymnast at Cal State Fullerton, then coached Cal State Northridge to a Division II national championship in 1982... graduated from William S. Hart (Calif.) High School in Santa Clarita…was committed to University of Portland, but chose to sign with the Pittsburgh Pirates after being drafted in the fifth round of the 2011 MLB Draft.

2024

In his first season with the Dodgers, went 9-6 with a 3.49 ERA (52 ER/134.0 IP) in 22 starts with 168 strikeouts against 35 walks...Was the Dodgers’ sixth different Opening Day starter since 2019, joining Hyun-Jin Ryu, Dustin May, Clayton, Kershaw, Walker Buehler and Julio Urías and became the third Dodger player in the last 86 years to make his Dodger debut as the Opening Day starter (last: RHP Derek Lowe, 2005)...Named to his first All-Star team, going 8-5 with a 3.47 ERA (42 ER/109.0 IP) in 18 starts…did not appear due to injury...Had his best month in April, going 4-1 with a 2.81 ERA (10 ER/32.0 IP) in five starts with 45 strikeouts...Tossed double digit strikeouts six times during the season, including a season-high 14 on April 9 at MIN in 7.0 scoreless innings while giving up only three hits...Was out from July 9 to 24 with lower back tightness and Aug. 13 through the end of the season with right elbow tendinitis.

2023

In his final season with Tampa Bay, went 10-7 with a 3.53 ERA, allowing 47 runs in 120 innings with 162 strikeouts in a career-best 21 starts... He finished with career-highs in wins (10), starts (21), strikeouts (162), strikeouts per 9 (12.2) and innings pitched (120.0 IP)... Spent two months (March 27-May 27) on the injured list with a left oblique strain...On a rehab assignment with Triple-A Durham, tossed a 0-0 record with a 0.68 ERA (1 ER/13.1 IP), only giving up eight hits total and holding batters to a .170 average...Tossed a 4-3 record with a 3.18 ERA (20 ER/56.2 IP) at home compared to 6-4 with a 3.84 ERA (27 ER/63.1 IP) in 11 starts on the road…curveball held batters to a .095 batting average (10-for-105)…fired his fastball in 44% of his pitches, compared to 34% with his slider and 21% with the curveball.

2022

Reinstated on Sep 28 and made two starts to end the regular season, returning from Tommy John surgery…began throwing from the bullpen on July 23 and progressed to live batting practice on Aug 20…began a rehab assignment with Triple-A Durham on Sept. 7 and made four starts for the Bulls, allowing one run in seven IP with 14 SO...Underwent arthroscopic surgery on March 18 to remove loose bodies from his right ankle, performed by Dr. Aaron Mates in Tampa...On Aug. 26, signed a two-year extension through the 2024 season, replacing his final year of arbitration eligibility and first year of free agency...On Sept. 28 at CLE, returned from Tommy John surgery and made his first start since June 14, 2021 at CHW, going 471 days in between… threw 50 pitches (32 strikes) in 3 IP and his only hits allowed were a solo homer and single to start the third inning…threw first-pitch strikes to 81.2 pct. (9 of 11) of batters faced and reached 99 mph four times, including a maximum velocity of 99.4 mph against Josh Naylor in the second...On Oct, 3 at BOS, made his second start and recorded seven SO in 3.2 scoreless innings…the seven SO were the most by a Ray in a start of 3.2 IP (or fewer) since Glasnow (7) went three IP on Sept. 21, Started Game 2 of the AL Wild Card Series at CLE…yielded 2 hits (both singles) in five scoreless innings of a game that remained scoreless until the bottom of the 15th inning...Underwent arthroscopic surgery on Nov. 30 to remove loose bodies from his left knee, performed by Dr. Koco Eaton in St. Petersburg.

2021

Underwent Tommy John surgery Aug 4, performed by Dr. Keith Meister in Arlington...Left his June 14 start at CHW after 4 IP due to injury, ending his season…at the time of his injury, ranked second in the majors with 123 SO behind CLE Shane Bieber (130) and ranked sixth in the AL with a 2.66 ERA...Limited batters to a .083 avg. (7-for-84) in at-bats ending with the curveball…led the majors at the time of his injury with 58 SO on the curveball despite using the pitch only 13.7 pct. of the time...His breaking pitches combined for 143 swings-and-misses, third in the majors at the time of his injury behind CLE Shane Bieber (191) and LAD Clayton Kershaw (174)...Had double-digit strikeouts in all six home starts, totaling 66 SO in 43.2 IP at Tropicana Field…became the sixth pitcher in the Wild Card era (since 1995) with 10 SO (or more) in six straight home starts, joining MIN Johan Santana (8) in 2004, HOU Gerrit Cole (6) in 2019, WSH Max Scherzer (6) in 2018, HOU/ARI Randy Johnson (6) in 1998-99 and PHI Curt Schilling (6) in 1997...Had double-digit strikeouts and allowed five hits (or fewer) in his first five home starts…according to Stats LLC, the only other pitcher in the modern era (since 1900) to do that in five consecutive home starts at any point in a single season was MIN Johan Santana (6) in 2004...Became the first pitcher to start multiple 1-0 wins within the first 10 games of a season since NYM Tom Seaver in 1971...According to Stats LLC, became the third pitcher in the modern era (since 1900) to have at least 29 SO and no more than one run allowed over their first three starts of a season…joined PHI Curt Schilling (34-SO, 1-R) in 1998 and BOS Pedro Martínez (32-SO, 1-R) in 1998...Was the first Rays pitcher to go five IP (or more) and allow one run (or fewer) in each of his first four starts of a season…that stretch extended his unbeaten streak to 12 starts, going 7-0 over that span...Went 3-1 with a 1.67 ERA (37.2-IP, 7-ER) and 56 SO in six starts during April…according to the Elias Sports Bureau, it marked the 3rd-lowest ERA for a Rays traditional starter in his first six starts behind Chris Archer (1.64) in 2015 and Scott Kazmir (1.22) in 2008…ranked among AL leaders in April in ERA (3rd), strikeouts (3rd), opp avg. (.144, 1st), WHIP (0.80, 2nd), swings-and-misses (102, 3rd) and fWAR (1.7, 2nd)...His 56 SO in April were the most in club history, passing Chris Archer (43) in April 2016…they were 2nd for any calendar month in Rays history behind Chris Archer (58) in May 2017...Made his fifth straight Opening Day roster...On April 1 at MIA, took no decision in his first career Opening Day start and his only baserunner allowed in six IP was a 1st-inning infield single to Jesús Aguilar that left the bat at 76.1 mph…delivered the second scoreless outing for an Opening Day starter in franchise history, been tied since...On April 23 vs. TOR, struck out his first 2 BF in the 1st inning and last 2 BF of the frame…in between, allowed a single, RBI double, walk, three-run homer and single before his 2011 vs. DET (3rd inning)…according to the Elias Sports Bureau, became the second pitcher in major league history to strike out four batters and allow four runs in the same inning, joining PHI Doc White on July 21, 1902 at Brooklyn...On May 8 at OAK, suffered his first road loss since Sep 22, 2018, at TOR…snapped a streak of nine consecutive winning decisions, the longest road streak all-time by a Rays starter.

2020

In his second full season with the Rays, led the team with 57.1 IP and 91 SO and tied for 2nd with 5 wins behind Pete Fairbanks (6)...Went 5-0 with a 3.00 ERA (42-IP, 14-ER) and 64 SO in his last 7 starts beginning on Aug. 19…this stretch lowered his season ERA from 7.04 to 4.08…all 5 wins came over his last 6 starts...His active 5-game winning streak is his longest since winning a career-high six consecutive decisions from March 30–May 3, 2019... His 91 SO were 4th in the AL behind CLE Shane Bieber (122), CWS Lucas Giolito (97) and NYY Gerrit Cole (94)…min. 50 IP, his 14.28 SO/9 IP ratio led the majors and was the 2nd-highest in club history behind Brad Boxberger (14.47) in 2014...According to the Elias Sports Bureau, his 81 SO through his first 50 IP of the season were the 2nd-most by a starting pitcher in major league history behind CLE Shane Bieber (82) in 2020...Ended the season with 7 SO (or more) in eight straight starts, the longest such streak in club history…passed David Price (June 24–July 30, 2012) and Scott Kazmir (July 24–Aug 25, 2007)...Hasn’t lost on the road since Sept. 22, 2018, at TOR, going 7-0 with a 1.96 ERA (64.1-IP, 14-ER) in 12 starts since then…min. 10 starts, is the only pitcher in the majors that did not suffer a road loss during the 2019-20 seasons...Limited batters to a .120 avg. POSTSEASON: Went 2-3 with a 6.28 ERA (28.2-IP, 20-ER) in six starts…the Rays won his first three starts and lost the last three…yielded 9 HR, a major league record for a single postseason…the previous mark was 8 HR by HOU Justin Verlander in 2019 and LAD Clayton Kershaw in 2017...In Game 2 of the Wild Card Series vs. TOR, his only runs allowed in 6 IP were a pair of solo homers to Danny Jansen...In Game 2 of the ALDS vs. NYY, recorded a Rays postseason-record 10 SO…allowed a Giancarlo Stanton solo homer to lead off the 2nd inning and a 3-run homer to Stanton in the 4th...For the second consecutive postseason, started a decisive Game 5 of the ALDS opposite Gerrit Cole…according to Stats LLC, it marked the second time two starting pitchers matched up in two different “winner take all” games…they joined MIL Lew Burdette and NYY Don Larsen, who faced each other in Game 7 of the 1957 World Series and in Game 7 of the 1958 World Series…started on two days’ rest after throwing 93 pitches in 5 IP of Game 2…became the first pitcher in Rays postseason history to start two games on fewer than four days’ rest in between… faced the Yankees once through the batting order and did not allow a run or hit, but walked two and got two outs on 3-0 pitches...Suffered the loss in Game 4 of the ALCS at HOU and Games 1 and 5 of the World Series against the Dodgers…pitched to a 8.22 ERA (15.1-IP, 14-ER) and yielded 5 HR in those three starts.
